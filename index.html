Professional Organizations in the Philippines: National Privacy Commission (NPC)

Role: The NPC is the primary authority enforcing the Data Privacy Act of 2012 (RA 10173).
Functions: Promotes compliance with privacy laws to protect personal data. Investigates and addresses data breaches. Enhances public awareness of data privacy rights.
Significance: NPC ensures organizations adopt robust information security measures to protect personal information, aligning with international frameworks.

International Organization Governing Information Security

ISACA (Information Systems Audit and Control Association)

Role: A global nonprofit organization specializing in IT governance, risk management, and cybersecurity.
Functions: Provides certifications such as: CISM (Certified Information Security Manager): Focused on managing information security programs. CISA (Certified Information Systems Auditor): Emphasizing IT audits and controls. CRISC (Certified in Risk and Information Systems Control): Highlighting risk management and mitigation. Publishes frameworks like COBIT for IT governance.
Significance: ISACA equips professionals with skills to implement, manage, and audit robust information security systems.

Conclusion

In the Philippines, organizations like the National Privacy Commission, PH-CERT, CICC, and local chapters of ISACA and (ISC)² govern information security by enforcing laws and offering certifications. These organizations collectively enhance information security, fostering a safer digital environment globally and locally.
